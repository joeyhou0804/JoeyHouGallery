Columbia HUMA 1123 Masterpieces of Western Music
The Visualization of MUSIC

Columbia Music Humanities Interdisciplinary Creative Project

This is a Just Shapes & Beats (a video game) inspired video for the requirement of the Interdisciplinary Creative Project of Masterpieces of Western Music, a music humanities course provided at Columbia University.

- How did I organize/create the animations?
I used Adobe Animate CC 2020 to make these flash videos. All objects are made and animated independently. Most of them are designed based on personal understandings (some parts, such as the usage of colors and particular objects, are references to the original game). For every music piece, considerations have been put into the shape choices.

- Why did I choose the songs/pieces I animated?
All songs I used are from the course syllabus. Each of them represents an idea from the music history, including “the Medieval Era”, “the Classical Era”, and “European Modernism”. I planned to step out from the field of techno music used by the original game and to detail music aspects, such as the mood, instruments, and texture, other than pure beats. Using new types of music will avoid the existing knowledge and push me into the challenge of applying what has been taught in class.
